2010 NATIONAL CHAMPIONS!!!

WEST POINT BOXING THREE PEATS!

The West Point Boxing team hosted the 2010 National Collegiate Boxing Championships April 8-10. Over 30 schools qualified for the national tournament. West Point, the returning two-time national champions, only entered 10 of 12 weight classes.

The final night saw West Point leading the team standings by only eight points, with Navy a close second. Nine boxers advanced to the final night of competition which only has 12 bouts. The point’s margin quickly grew to be insurmountable as West Point boxers dominated five of nine opponents. CDT Andrew Manglicmot, ’12, F1, fought a hard fight but lost to a 3-2 decision to Navy opponent.

Then West Point went on a winning streak. Cleaning up on the 125lb, 132lb, 139lb, 147lb and 165lb weight classes. CDT Jamal Robinson ’10, B4, fought the two-time defending champion at 175lbs and lost to a 4-1 decision. CDT Tyler Elliot, ’12, C3, also lost to a 4-1 decision. CDT Seth King, ’11, G2, fought a valiant fight against a very tough hard hitting opponent from UNLV and lost to a 4-1 decision.

The West Point Boxing team won its third straight national championship and beat Navy and Air Force handily with a final team standing of: West Point 63 points; Navy 45 points; and Air Force 19 points. West Point had five individual champions: 125lbs CDT Danilo Garcia ’11, C3; 132lbs CDT Johnny Gracia, ’11, D4; 139lbs CDT Terrell Anthony, ’11, D4; 147lbs CDT Steven Henao-Escobar, ’11, A1, and 165lbs CDT Ryle Stous, ’10, H3. West Point boxers also claimed Most Valuable Boxer at 147lbs and below (Terrell Anthony) and Most Valuable Boxer 156lbs and up (Ryle Stous).
